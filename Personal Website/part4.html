Plot

After making up his mind to kill Hoyt, Jason, with the help of Agent Willis, gets to the Southern Rook Island. He also gets a new equipment, the wing suit which is very useful in the game.

There, Jason meets with an old acquaintance of Willis, Sam Becker, who is working undercover among Hoyt's soldiers.

Sam Becker

He gives instructions on how to get close to Hoyt. First, Jason must steal a soldier uniform and blend in with Hoyt's men. During that mission, Jason finds out some soldiers are planning something Hoyt doesn't like. Considering his chance, Jason tries to find out more about the secret plan to reveal it to Hoyt. Only then he will have the chance to meet Hoyt who will thank him in person. In Hoyt's base, Jason meets someone unexpected...

"Uh, well. Do you know the real secret to keeping employees happy? Fear." -Hoyt

To save his little brother, Riley, Jason must now make double sure to kill the man capturing him, Hoyt.

After sabotaging some of Hoyt's bases, Sam and Jason try to kill him in a friendly poker game. However things..... don't turn out well.

"So, name your price. Everybody has one. You're all puppets and we pull the strings. The system was designed to work that way." -Hoyt

More unexpected things awaits Jason at Citra's temple....

"I've killed so many people I've lost count. I can't come back from this. I'm a monster. I can feel the anger inside me. But I am still, somewhere inside me, more than that. Better than that." -Jason

Conclusion

I love Far Cry 3. It's one of the very first PC games that I played. But it will be one of the last games I will forget. It showed me what a good video game is. Compelling characters, a great plot, fun gameplay, many enjoyable side activities to do, and many more; these things make Far Cry 3 truly one of the best games of all time. I hope everyone who contributed to the making of Far Cry 3 is living their best lives knowing that they created such a masterpeice. I'm also glad that I now have someone to whom I've told about this fantastic game. Thank you ivy, for journeying with me through this insanity.
